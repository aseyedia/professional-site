---
title: Amygdala & Anterior Cingulate Transcriptomes in Bipolar Disorder
tag: research
summary: Amygdala and anterior cingulate transcriptomes from individuals with bipolar disorder reveal downregulated neuroimmune and synaptic pathways. Nature Neuroscience, 2022.
externalUrl: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41593-022-01024-6
journal: Nature Neuroscience
date: 2022-03-07
abstract: >-
  This study explored how gene expression changes in two key brain
  regions, the amygdala and the anterior cingulate cortex, contribute to
  the pathology of bipolar disorder. The research identified
  downregulated neuroimmune and synaptic pathways and pinpointed
  specific genes influenced by bipolar disorder-associated genetic
  variants. These findings provide critical insights into the
  biological mechanisms underlying bipolar disorder and its genetic
  risk factors.
role: >-
  I conducted the Z-score analysis and transcriptome-wide association
  studies (TWAS), integrating SNP datasets with RNA-seq data to
  identify transcriptional mechanisms linked to bipolar disorder risk.
  This involved harmonizing genetic coordinates, computing gene-level
  weights, and uncovering gene expression patterns associated with
  genome-wide significant loci.
