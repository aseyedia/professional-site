---
title: Spatial & Single-Cell Mapping of Castleman Disease
tag: research
summary: Spatial and single-cell map of Castleman Disease revealing key stromal cell types and cytokine pathways. Nature Communications, 2025.
externalUrl: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-61214-1
journal: Nature Communications
date: 2025-07-01
abstract: >-
  This research investigated Castleman disease (CD), a rare
  lymphoproliferative disorder characterized by abnormal lymph node
  enlargement. While inflammatory molecules like IL-6 and VEGF were known
  to drive the disease, the specific cellular sources of these cytokines
  and the differences between disease subtypes (unicentric vs multicentric
  CD) remained unclear. The study used spatial proteomics, single-nuclei
  RNA sequencing, and spatial transcriptomics to analyze lymph node
  samples from 22 patients, creating a comprehensive cellular atlas of
  Castleman disease. The findings revealed that stromal cells, rather
  than immune cells, are the primary drivers of disease pathology, with
  different stromal cell types (follicular dendritic cells, T-zone
  reticular cells, and perivascular reticular cells) predominating in
  different disease subtypes.
role: >-
  I performed the computational analysis of the single-nuclei RNA
  sequencing data, processing 50,117 nuclei across all patient samples.
  The analysis included quality control, ambient RNA removal using
  SoupX, data integration with Seurat's RPCA method, doublet detection
  using DoubletFinder, batch correction with ComBat, and cell type
  annotation. This work identified cell population changes between
  disease subtypes, mapped pathway activation states, and determined
  the cellular sources of disease-driving cytokines. The transcriptomic
  analysis validated spatial findings and provided insights into
  stromal cell-immune interactions that may inform future therapeutic
  approaches.
