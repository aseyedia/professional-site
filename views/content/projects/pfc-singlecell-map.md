---
title: Single-Cell & Spatial Map of the Human Prefrontal Cortex
tag: research
summary: A data-driven single-cell and spatial transcriptomic map of the human prefrontal cortex. Science, 2024.
externalUrl: https://www.science.org/doi/abs/10.1126/science.adh1938
journal: Science
date: 2024-05-24
abstract: >-
  This study developed a high-resolution molecular map of the
  dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (DLPFC), combining single-cell and
  spatial transcriptomics to identify distinct spatial domains and cell
  type compositions across cortical layers. The findings provided
  insights into molecular neuroanatomy, cell-cell interactions, and
  genetic risks for neuropsychiatric disorders, including
  schizophrenia, autism, and depression.
role: >-
  I piloted the use of <a href="https://github.com/broadinstitute/Tangram"
  target="_blank" rel="noopener">Tangram</a>, an advanced computational
  tool for spatial transcriptomics, to align single-cell and spatial
  data with high precision. This involved optimizing Tangram's
  application to our dataset, validating its predictions with
  immunofluorescence reference data, and refining the spatial mapping
  of cell types and molecular interactions.
